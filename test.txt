Yes, in PrimeNG v18 follow the new structure: remove label from <p-radioButton> and add a separate <label> using inputId.

Use this:

<div class="flex gap-4">
  <div class="flex items-center">
    <p-radioButton
      name="contractTemplateAvailable"
      [value]="true"
      formControlName="contractTemplateAvailable"
      inputId="contractTemplateAvailableYes"
      adminSelenium="crud_frm_event_contractTemplateAvailable_true">
    </p-radioButton>
    <label for="contractTemplateAvailableYes" class="ml-2">Yes</label>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-center">
    <p-radioButton
      name="contractTemplateAvailable"
      [value]="false"
      formControlName="contractTemplateAvailable"
      inputId="contractTemplateAvailableNo"
      adminSelenium="crud_frm_event_contractTemplateAvailable_false">
    </p-radioButton>
    <label for="contractTemplateAvailableNo" class="ml-2">No</label>
  </div>
</div>

Also make sure your form value is boolean:

contractTemplateAvailable: [null]
// or
contractTemplateAvailable: [true]

Main change from your old code:

label="Yes"

should become:

<inputId="contractTemplateAvailableYes">
<label for="contractTemplateAvailableYes">Yes</label>

For PrimeNG v18, this is the safer and documented way.
